Why Disneyland Means So Much to Me

I talk about Disneyland a lot — like, a suspicious amount for someone who does not currently live anywhere near Anaheim. But the thing is, Disneyland isn’t just a “theme park” to me. It’s this weird, magical comfort blanket woven out of music loops, dark rides, churro scent pipelines, and an almost concerning level of nostalgia.

The Feeling of Stepping Inside a Memory

Every time I walk under the train station and onto Main Street, my brain enters that “you’re home again, buddy” mode. It’s the only place where I immediately relax, even though there are approximately 48,000 strollers sprinting directly at my ankles.

There’s just something about Disneyland’s scale — the intimacy of it. Everything is human-sized, charming, and intentionally imperfect. Disneyland feels lived in. World-built. Like somebody obsessively polished and repainted an entire world just to make people smile for a day.

Tomorrowland, My Beloved

Tomorrowland is straight-up my aesthetic spirit animal. The white, silver, and blue color palette? The geometric shapes? The retro-future optimism? Inject that directly into my drywall. If I had the money, my entire room would look like “a retired Star Tours pilot’s apartment.”

Even the weird contradictions of Tomorrowland — the fact that it’s both futuristic and outdated at the same time — somehow makes me love it more. It’s like, yeah, maybe the future never looks exactly how we imagine it. But that doesn’t make imagining it any less fun.

New Orleans Square Lives In My Head 24/7

I swear New Orleans Square is one of the most beautiful themed lands ever built. Every brick, every lamp, every balcony is just… chef’s kiss. Haunted Mansion and Pirates of the Caribbean are two of my all-time comfort rides. I know the smell of Pirates could probably be bottled and sold as “Eau de Questionable Water Treatment,” but listen — it works.

And don’t even get me started on the nighttime ambiance. New Orleans Square at night feels like stumbling into a dream you’ve had before but forgot.

The Music, The Atmosphere, The… Everything

Disneyland is one of the few places where sound design genuinely feels like sorcery. The loops, the transitions between lands, the way everything crossfades so perfectly — bro, this is why I’m making entire apps dedicated to recreating these soundscapes.

There’s a reason “Disneyland ambience” videos have like ten million hours of watch time. People want to feel calm. People want to feel transported. Disneyland does that effortlessly.

It’s the Place I Associate With Hope

I think the real reason Disneyland hits so hard for me is because it represents escape, creativity, safety, and possibility all at once.

There were a lot of moments in my life where I felt kind of lost or overwhelmed, and Disneyland — even just in videos, photos, or soundtracks — was this little lighthouse I could stare at to feel grounded again. It’s comfort. It’s warmth. It’s a reminder that magic isn’t real but also totally is.

The Future

One day, I want to go back with people I care about — friends, family, whoever decides they can put up with me screaming “THAT’S THE PETER PAN MUSIC LOOP!!” at random times. I want to stay until they gently kick me out.

Disneyland isn’t just a “favorite park.” It’s a place that shaped my imagination, my aesthetic, and honestly a big chunk of who I am.

And the second I walk under that train station again, I already know I’m gonna tear up a little. It is what it is. I contain multitudes. One of them cries at theme parks.
